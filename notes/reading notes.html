Labour General reading
Norio et al. (2023). Wage Functions in China and Eastern Europe: A Large-Scale Comparative Meta Analysis. 2023/4/19.
  • Background: Since 1990s, both Eastern Europe and China have changed significantly: market-orientation, globalization, technological innovation comparable to that in developed countries.
  • Question: How have wage functions changed since the reforms?
  • Data and Method: 6453 estimates reported in 216 previous research works; Meta-analysis
  • Findings: 1) Both China and Eastern Europe are having flattening wage functions w.r.t experience - the impact of work experience on wage level was gradually diminishing; the wage seniority system was dissolving over time.
    2) The impact of education and gender on wage levels in China have been gradually increasing toward the present, while those in Eastern Europe have been declining.
  • Mechanisms: 1) Experience: Market transition policies are accompanied by early retirement, the accelerated obsolescence of traditional skills, and accelerated early turnover.
    2) Education: Higher education levels come with economic growth - human capital bringing higher remuneration to workers and higher skills to firms, which has further encouraged increased human capital investment.
    3) Gender: Industries relying on unskilled/semi-skilled labor have been preserved. Women’s share of total employment in labor-intensive sectors is high, preserving the gender gap.
