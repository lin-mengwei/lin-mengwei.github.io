Essays in the Economics of Education:
Throughout the Journey







General Introduction

Motivation

In the journey of life, education plays a pivotal role in shaping the trajectory of individuals, from their early stages in school education to their later stages as experienced workers. Understanding the dynamic relationship between education, human capital development, labor market outcomes, and economic growth is crucial for comprehending the broader implications of educational systems on individuals and societies across the life cycle.

General Introduction

Overview

In this PhD dissertation, I will explore in three independent papers three interconnected facets that span different stages of life, examining student incentives during university years and extending to labor market risks faced by individuals in later stages of their careers.

1    Does Raising Costs Boost Student Effort?

Background and Motivation

In OECD countries, for example, the amount of per-student spending surpasses that of other levels of education, and these public investments have continued to rise at a faster pace than the increase in enrolment rates. However, the utilization of these funds does not seem to be as efficient as expected, as evidenced by recent data showing that only 39% of undergraduates in OECD countries graduate on time and that more than a fifth of enrolled students do not manage to graduate after all (OECD, 2022).

The Spanish government increased university tuition fees in the following years in order to reduce expenses in the context of the European debt crisis.
