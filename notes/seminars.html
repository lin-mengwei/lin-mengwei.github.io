Maria Lombardi. The effect of high dismissal protection on bureaucratic turnover and productivity.
  • Question: What is the impact of high dismissal protection for civic servants on turnover and productivity (in the context of public school teachers in Chile)?
  • Definitions:
    1. high dismissal protection: a law in 2015 granting a permanent contract to temporary teachers with a minimum "seniority" (3-4 years) by 2015 (policy change)
    2. productivity: standardized test scores of students
  • Background: teachers get hired by municipalities as permanent or temporary workers, having the same salary/benefits/job but different dismissal protection. Permanent ones do better in the standardized teacher evaluation.
  • Data: admin data on all teaching positions in Chile, 2003-2016 (Chilean Ministry of Education)
  • Identification: Diff-in-diff (teachers with 2 vs 3 years of experience in 2014)
  • Results: High dismissal protection can be a double-edged sword. It:
    1) reduces turnover by 25 percent in the first two years. (Heterogeneity: The reduction is statistically significant among teachers both at the bottom and top of the distribution of baseline performance - average quality unchanged: keeping both best and worst ones) and
    2) decreases the learning of students taught by teachers with low baseline performance, but there is no effect on overall student test scores.
  • Lit Review/Contribution: personnel economics of the state, and a common but barely examined policy
David Rueda. Crime or Redistribution: Fairness, Effort and Redistribution Preferences.
  • Question: Asymmetry of opportunity/Procedural Fairness → redistribution OR crime?
    How does asymmetry in opporyunnity impact preferences for redisibution and both then affect policing as crime deterrent (amonst the wealthy) and the willingness to commit crime (amongst the poor)?
  • Background: In a society where you know your income is based on effort while some other people's are based on luck.
  • Method: Lab experiment - one group earning their income through effort; one through luck with efforts (costs); one through pure luck. People are divided into rich(80) and poor(20) groups.
    1. Earners work for 15 min first to earn their endowments depending either by work or luck
    2. (pure luck) Inheriters come in and get their endowments (some got 80, some 20)
    3. People decide the level of redistribution they prefer, then redistribution relized.
    4. Rich people choose investment in policing; poor people choose whether to steal or not.
  • Results: 1. Distribution preferences: In general, with more luck as a determinant of income, people choose a higher rate of redistribution. Poor people choose higher redistribution.
    2. Crime and Policing: Real policing investment is lower than optimal. Poor particiants guess that policing is higher than it really is.
  • Contribution: They go from a detailed lit summary to three contributions and then expand them!
    1. distinguishing two types of fairness: reward for work vs for talent/performance
    2. distinguishing fairness of procedure vs of outcomes
Belén Rodríguez. Fertility and Relationship Quality.
  • Question: the impact of first child birth on the relationship quality of couples
  • Mechanism: First child is an shock to home production, couples pushed to rearrange their time.
    1. reduction in time spent together: going out, kissing, ...
    2. individual time arrangements: weekly number of hours for paid/unpaid work
  • Background: understanding why some pro-fertility pocilies fail (even leading to divorce)
  • Definition:
    1. Relationship Quality: love (non-pecuniary component of individuals's welfare of being coupled); Measurement: use factor analysis to construct RQ
  • Data: Understanding Society, 2009-2021 (7,100 couples)
  • Method: Event study comparing people w/w.o children
  • Results:
    1. There is a sharp and permanent decrease in RQ immediately after birth, both for fathers and mothers.
    2. Richer couples have lowset falls in RQ: outsource partly the shock.
    3. Less specialized couples resent the arrival of the first child
  • Contribution: No mentioned
Ria Ivandic. Gender Gaps from Labour Market Shocks.
  • Question: Gender differences in labor market recovery following job loss - What are the effects of male vs female job loss on labor market outcomes? When are gender gaps mitigated or exacerbated? What explains all this?
  • Background: Covid-19 Job Loss Disproportionately Affects Women.
  • Data: admin data from Demnark (reason to choose: good data quality), 1995-2007 - firm-level, employer-employee matched, and individual level data ❗
  • Identification: event study - comparing workers who experienced a plant closure with similar (matched) ones who did not across years before and after the closure. (Y - employment status, (relative and absolute) earnings)
  • Results:
    1. Women face greater risk of unemployment 2 years after job loss (no gender gap in labor supply)
    2. Gender gaps are the largest among disadvantaged groups and parents.
    3. Initial sorting across sector, occupation, firms and plants doesn't explain the gender gap; gaps in human capital explains part of it, but less in the presence of children.
  • Lit Review/Contribution: same field, but different strands (different causal paths)
