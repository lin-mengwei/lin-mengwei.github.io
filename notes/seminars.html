Felipe Carozzi. Blaming Your Predecessor: Government Turnover and External Financial Assistance.
  • Observation: Newly elected parties can blame previous gov for not asking for assistance.
  • Hypothesis: Newly elected governments are more likely to request financial support.
  • Mechanism: Requesting assitance reveals info on poor state of public finances and quality of re-elected incumbents.
  • Broader Question: Re-election → External financial support
  • Outcomes: existing funding agreements (NOT the requests!)
  • Data Source: IMF Financing Programs (Country-level); Plan de Pago a Proveedores from Spain (municipal-level)
  • Sample: IMF data: 1992-2019;
  • Empirical Strategies: 1) Descriptives: IMF Financing ~ government turnover (❓endogeneity - e.g., two-way causality, confounder); 2) RD - cutoff from close election (❓external validity)
  • Results: 1) party change is associated with 4% more in chance for requesting IMF financing.
    2) propensity for a new gov to request for assistance is over 30%!
  • Lit Review: Pay attention to how he sorts it!

Maria Lombardi. The effect of high dismissal protection on bureaucratic turnover and productivity.
  • Question: What is the impact of high dismissal protection for civic servants on turnover and productivity (in the context of public school teachers in Chile)?
  • Definitions:
    1. high dismissal protection: a law in 2015 granting a permanent contract to temporary teachers with a minimum seniority by 2015 (policy change)
    2. productivity: standardized test scores of students
  • Background: teachers get hired by municipalities as permanent or temporary workers, having the same salary/benefits/job but different dismissal protection. Permanent ones do better in the standardized teacher evaluation.
  • Data: admin data
  • Identification: Diff-in-diff (teachers affected vs not affected before and after)
  • Results: High dismissal protection can be a double-edged sword. It:
    1) reduces turnover by 25 percent in the first two years. (Heterogeneity: The reduction is statistically significant among teachers both at the bottom and top of the distribution of baseline performance - average quality unchanged: keeping both best and worst ones) and
    2) decreases the learning of students taught by teachers with low baseline performance, but there is no effect on overall student test scores.
  • Lit Review/Contribution: Pay attention to how they sort it!
    1. contributions to personnel economics of the state,and a common but barely examinedpolicy
