My name is Mengwei Lin. I am a PhD candidate at the University of Barcelona (UB) School of Economics and the Barcelona Institute of Economics (IEB, Public Policies), where I work with Andreu Arenas and Jenifer Ruiz-Valenzuela. From Spring 2024, I will participate in the Course Program for Doctoral Students in Economics of Education by the Swiss Leading House VPET-ECON at the University of Zurich.

My interests mainly lie in the Economics of Education, as well as Labor Economics, and the Economics of Demography (esp. the aging society); on the method side, I am interested in Causal Inference (policy evaluation) and Big Data in Social Sciences .

Prior to Barcelona, I worked at the China Development Institute (Research Center for Public Policy, with Wang Mei) and as a freelance translator, after obtaining my Master's Degree in Economics (with Distinction) from the SGPE Programme at The University of Edinburgh.

Since 2017, I have been a certified English-Chinese translator with a specialization in book translation in the Social Sciences. (Latest: Chinese version of- What's Wrong with Economics? A Primer for the Perplexed, Robert Skidelsky).

My first name "Mengwei" has two syllables, meng (pronunciation here) and wei (pronounced as the word "way").
